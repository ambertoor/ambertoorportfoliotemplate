My Coding Journey

I began "coding" a few years ago however I lacked motivation and struggled with many of the key concepts. So, I gave up. Fast forward to just last year during the pandemic, I gave coding another chance and joined the Girls Who Code organization. I had the fortune of meeting with women from across the globe and was able to see firsthand the benefits of learning to code and what the opportunities it had produced for them. I was inspired. So, I decided to apply for the Rice University coding course through Girls Who Code (they were offering a scholarship) and I was accepted! I'm not saying I'm definitely going to go into the tech industry. (I'm more of an fashion person) but I now believe that learning to code should be an essential part of our school curiculums and am delighted to say that I will be pursuing more coding classes and projects in the near future.
